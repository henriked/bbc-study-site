Experts urge to put growth first because the costs of climate action are damaging economies

Some experts warn that aggressive climate policy is outpacing what economies can absorb, risking jobs and living standards.
10 hours ago
Peter Wilson
Environment and tourism correspondent
Wind turbines near coastal farmland in the UK.

A number of economists are urging governments to slow climate regulation, arguing that rapid green mandates are placing a burden on businesses and households, which are already squeezed by high inflation and low growth.

“The business-as-usual baseline, against which costs of action are measured, conveys a profoundly misleading message to policymakers that there is an alternative option in which fossil fuels are consumed in ever greater quantities without any negative consequences to economic growth itself.”, says Lord Nicholas Stern, from the London School of Economics, in the UK.

Energy costs across Europe have risen sharply in recent years. This is partly driven by the accelerated phase-out of fossil fuels. Experts argue that poorer households and energy-intensive industries are bearing a disproportionate share of the transition costs.

“I’m all for looking after the planet, but someone must look after us first. My overheads have gone up so much I’m not sure I can keep the business going.” Sandra Howarth, 49, small business owner, Amsterdam.
