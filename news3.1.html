Economists warn that governments must limit immigration to protect worker’s wages

Researchers say that tighter controls on legal residency would ease pressure on low-income earners and ease strain on public services.
23 hours ago
Jack Thompson
Political editor
Migration laws become increasingly restrictive across Europe.

Economists say governments should tighten the rules governing legal entry and settlement, arguing that high volumes of low-skilled migration suppress wages for vulnerable workers and increase pressure on housing and public services.

"The largest loss is probably the wage drop suffered by the workers who face competition in the labour market. My rule of thumb is that if immigration increases the number of workers by 10 percent, the wage of workers probably drops by about 3 percent."says George Borjas,professor at Harvard Kennedy School.

Professor Borjas's research suggests that while immigration increases overall GDP, the net benefit to existing workers - after accounting for wage suppression - is small. According to his calculations, native workers collectively lose around $133 billion a year in wages to immigrant labour competition.

"Although immigration makes the aggregate economy larger, the actual net benefit accruing to natives is small... There is little evidence indicating that immigration creates large net gains for native-born Americans."Prof. George Borjas, Harvard Kennedy School
